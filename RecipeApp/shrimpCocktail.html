Shrimp Cocktail

If you've ever wondered: is shrimp cocktail served raw? The answer is no—the shrimp is boiled until pink and cooked through, then chilled before serving. If you’re looking to switch up the classic preparation, check out our roasted shrimp cocktail recipe. Our recipe is written for raw, thawed shrimp, but you can absolutely use frozen shrimp here—just place the frozen shrimp directly in the pot, and keep in mind that the time it takes to come to a boil and cook through will likely be a bit longer than the instructions below.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

instructions

1. Fill a large bowl halfway with ice and add water to cover; set aside. Cut one lemon into wedges.

2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the peeled and deveined shrimp and cook until they turn pink and opaque, about 3-4 minutes.

3. Drain the shrimp immediately and transfer them to the prepared ice water to cool rapidly. Once cooled, drain again.

4. In a small bowl, mix together ketchup, horseradish, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, hot sauce, salt, and pepper to make the cocktail sauce.

5. Serve the chilled shrimp on a platter with the cocktail sauce. Garnish with fresh parsley and lemon wedges.

ingredients

1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined

1/2 cup ketchup

1/4 cup prepared horseradish

1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice

1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce

1/2 teaspoon hot sauce (adjust to taste)

Salt and pepper to taste

Fresh parsley for garnish

Lemon wedges for serving
