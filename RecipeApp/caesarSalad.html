Caesar Salad

There's a reason that in the 90 years since its invention, the Caesar salad has become the default second salad option at every single major restaurant chain in the country. We all know what it is: chopped romaine lettuce and garlicky croutons, tossed in a creamy dressing made with eggs, olive oil, lemon, Parmesan, Worcestershire sauce, and anchovies. Even when mass-produced, this combination of savory, creamy, tangy, and crunchy ingredients is tasty stuff.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

0 min.

serving

4 servings

instructions

1. Wash and chop the romaine lettuce into bite-sized pieces.

2. Prepare homemade croutons by toasting bread cubes in olive oil until golden.

3. In a large bowl, combine the romaine lettuce and croutons.

4. Drizzle Caesar dressing over the salad and toss gently.

5. Grate Parmesan cheese on top and garnish with additional croutons.

ingredients

1 head of romaine lettuce

1 cup homemade croutons

1/2 cup Caesar dressing

1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Salt and pepper to taste
